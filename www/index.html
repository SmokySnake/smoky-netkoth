Network King of the Hill 
(NetKotH)

Setup:

1. Set your host and attack box to the static IP you were provided, in range 10.20.30.10-100/24. IMPORTANT: If you leave either your host or attack box on DHCP, they will show up as attackable IPs. 
Do so at your own peril. 
ALTERNATIVELY: If everyone agrees then setting up your own flag on your attack box could be a fun way to detect and defend attacks to your own machine. Use the <personal>name</personal> tags to let the scoreboard know this is a 
flag on a personal box. No one is then to hack this IP, but we didt say anything about not MitM to block or steal points ;).

2. Set your attack box VM to bridge mode, using your ethernet adapter

3. Plug in the ethernet adaptor and try pinging 10.20.30.1, accessing this page at 10.20.30.1:8000. Targets should be reachable.

Scoring:

1. The teams will be given a range of IPs running web servers with vulnerabilities to attack, their goal is to deface the front page on each. Expect there to be Linux and Windows boxes, and maybe some surprises. >:)  
The IPs for this game will be in the range of:
10.20.30.101 – 10.20.30.200
Versions will be switching during the game. 

2. Teams try to put up their own defacements at http://IP:80/flag.html, take down other people's defacements, and lock down the box to keep other teams out. NOTE that the flag may not exist at this location, 
it's your job to make it exist before you can score points. The team’s name must be in the <team>name</team> tag when they deface the site for the scoring system to register it, but they can change the page however they like.

3. Once per minute (more or less) the scoring software will see who currently owns the site, and score it.

4. Referees will work as a blue team to occasionally step in and change things on the target IPs. Fix the defacement, patch, roll back changes, switch Operating Systems, etc, just to even the playing field and make things interesting.

Rules:

1. Only penetrate the hosts at the given IPs, not the scoring box (10.20.30.1) or other contestant's boxes. XSS of a player may be ok as long as it’s not destructive. >:)
2. DoS and network routing/traffic attacks are allowed, even on the traffic coming to and from contestants’ and the scoring box.
3. Stay on the NetKotH network while attacking.
4. We may change/add other rules at will.

You may view the current scores by surfing to 10.20.30.1:8000
